The Ryan & Robbie books
tell the story of...

..the adventures ...

“

Ryan and Robbie loved to go on adventures, and one day they wanted to go for a bike ride.

Their mom always took great care of the boys. She knew that sometimes Ryan rode his bike so fast, he looked as if he were flying.

And even though Robbie rode his bike slower, she knew that he was curious about everything and often liked to get off his bike and play. ”

... of multicultural brothers...

“

Ryan and Robbie are brothers.

Ryan is two years older, and sometimes Robbie calls him Ge Ge, which means "elder brother" in Chinese.

They were born in California and now they live in Washington State... Their mother is from China. ”

..as they learn values ...

“

Ryan and Robbie teach their little brother
the Kung Fu way of thinking.

Ryan says, “To do Kung Fu, we must be respectful.”

Robbie says, “To do Kung Fu, we must listen carefully.”

Together, they say, “To master Kung Fu, we must not make trouble. We must practice self-defense!”

”

... from past generations ...

“

Ryan and Robbie love Kung Fu!

They practice every day.

The brothers learned Kung Fu from their lao yé when they visited China last year.

(Lao yé means “grandfather” in Chinese.)

”

..and become closer as a family.

“

"Come on in and give me a hug. It's time for lunch," their mom said.

The boys gave her a big hug, washed their hands, then sat down with their mom and dad to pray as a family.

They ate pot stickers, corn on the cob, spinach and carrots. The boys told their mom all about their bike ride...Then they each whispered in their mother's ear and told her exactly how Lao Lao's secrets had helped them.

”
